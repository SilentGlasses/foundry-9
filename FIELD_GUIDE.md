## [ I. THE SUBSTRATE: MATTER & VOID ]

The foundation of every Protocol is the "Void-State." In the Great Silence, standard blacks became "muddy", filled with digital artifacts and visual noise. Our Smiths had to engineer a new standard of darkness to house our signals.

#### MATERIAL: IONIZED OBSIDIAN

- **Extraction Site:** Deep-cache server wreckage; Sector 7.
- **Properties:** A zero-reflectance black that creates a "visual vacuum." 
- **Tactical Use:** This is the primary background for all Foundry-9 builds. It doesn't just sit behind your code; it isolates it, protecting the user's focus from the surrounding Static.

#### MATERIAL: CINDER-LEAD

- **Extraction Site:** Abandoned fabrication cooling racks.
- **Properties:** A heavy, textured gray that suggests weight and history.
- **Tactical Use:** Used for UI borders and secondary text. It provides the "industrial" feel of a physical machine, grounding the digital interface in something that feels tangible.

## [ II. THE EXOTICS: FREQUENCY FRAGMENTS ]

These are the "Colors" reclaimed from the pre-Collapse era. Each one required a "Stabilization Chamber" to prevent the hex codes from drifting back into the Static.

#### STATIC-CYAN

- **Classification:** High-Frequency Radiant.
- **Lore:** Recovered from a life-support terminal in the sub-basement of a dead hospital. It was the only light left on in the building.
- **Behavior:** It pierces through visual noise with surgical precision. It was designed to be seen when everything else is failing.

#### AMBER-PULSE

- **Classification:** Thermal-Kinetic.
- **Lore:** Harvested from the cooling fans of a Sector-9 furnace during a high-heat cast. It carries the "glow" of the forge itself.
- **Behavior:** Warm but aggressive. It draws the eye to critical alerts without causing optical fatigue.

#### VOID-VIOLET

- **Classification:** Sub-Aetheric.
- **Lore:** A "ghost" frequency found in the background radiation of a deleted database. 
- **Behavior:** Muted and deep. It creates a sense of immense digital distance, perfect for long-form coding sessions where the world outside needs to disappear.

## [ III. THE STABILIZATION PROCESS ]

Every material listed above must undergo the **Triple-Pass Tempering** before it is added to a YAML schema:

1.  **THE ISOLATION:** The frequency is stripped of all "Static" artifacts.
2.  **THE POLISH:** The contrast ratio is tuned to a 4.5:1 minimum against Ionized Obsidian.
3.  **THE CASTING:** The hex code is locked into a Warp-native protocol, ensuring it doesn't "bleed" into the neighboring pixels.
